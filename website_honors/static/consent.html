Consent to Participate in Research

1. Summary of the Research

You are invited to take part in a research study. The purpose of this study is to understand how people perform simple computer control tasks and how their actions help train artificial intelligence systems.

If you join the study, you will play a few simple online games.

Your total time in the study will be about 15 minutes.

The main risks are mild frustration or fatigue and a very small risk of loss of confidentiality.

About 100 people will take part. Participation is voluntary and you may stop at any time.

Any work performed on MTurk can be linked to your public profile page. Data collected from this study may be collected and used by Amazon per its privacy agreement. Your MTurk Worker ID will not be shared with anyone.

In accordance with MTurk policies, your payment may be rejected if the HIT was not completed correctly or you did not follow instructions. MTurk Worker IDs will only be collected for the purposes of distributing compensation and will not be associated with your survey response. However, it may be possible to determine your identity if you have a publicly available Amazon profile.

This study contains a number of checks to make sure you are finishing the tasks honestly and completely. As long as you read the instructions and complete the tasks, your HIT will be approved. If you fail these checks, your HIT will be rejected.

2. Why is this research being done?

This study examines how humans provide demonstrations that computers can learn from. This may help improve future artificial intelligence systems used in technology, transportation, and robotics.

3. Duration

Your participation will last about 15 minutes in a single online session.

4. What will happen if I take part?

  1. You will read this consent form and choose whether to participate
  2. You will receive instructions for a simple browser-based game and have some practice session for each game
  3. You will play the games while your actions and scores will be recorded
  4. You will receive a completion code

5. Risks

  • Mild fatigue or frustration
  • Minimal confidentiality risk

6. Privacy

No names or direct identifiers will be collected. Data is stored securely and used only for research.

7. Voluntary Participation

You may stop at any time with no penalty.
